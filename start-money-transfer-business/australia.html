Regulatory Framework

Understanding AUSTRAC & the Australian Regulatory Framework

In Australia, money transfer businesses — known as Remittance Network Providers (RNPs) — must register with AUSTRAC under the Anti-Money Laundering and Counter-Terrorism Financing Act 2006 (AML/CTF Act).

Operating without AUSTRAC registration is a criminal offence. The registration process requires a fit and proper assessment, a documented AML/CTF programme, and appointment of a compliance officer.

Regulator
AUSTRAC (Australian Transaction Reports and Analysis Centre)
Legislation
AML/CTF Act 2006 & AML/CTF Rules 2007
Entity Type
Remittance Network Provider (RNP) or Independent Remittance Dealer (IRD)
Registration Time
Typically 3–6 months from application submission

AUSTRAC Registration Requirements

1
Fit & Proper Assessment
Directors and key persons assessed for criminal history, financial history, and competency. RemitSo helps you prepare all required documentation.
2
AML/CTF Programme
A documented programme covering customer due diligence, transaction monitoring, employee training, and record-keeping. RemitSo designs this for you.
3
AUSTRAC Online Registration
Submit registration through AUSTRAC Online portal. RemitSo's compliance team guides the entire application.
4
Ongoing Obligations
Annual AML/CTF compliance reports, suspicious matter reports (SMRs), threshold transaction reports (TTRs). RemitSo automates all of these.
