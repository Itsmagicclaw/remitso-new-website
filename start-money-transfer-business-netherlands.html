Regulatory Framework

Netherlands — What the Law Requires

Understanding your regulatory obligations before you launch protects your business and your customers.

01
Regulator
De Nederlandsche Bank (DNB) is the lead regulatory authority. Oversees authorisation, ongoing supervision, and enforcement for payment institutions operating in Netherlands.
02
Licensing Framework
The Netherlands regulates money transfer under the Wwft (Wet ter voorkoming van witwassen en financieren van terrorisme) for AML, and the Wft (Wet op het financieel toezicht) for licensing. DNB supervises payment institutions. EU passporting applies.
03
AML Requirements
Full AML/CTF programme required — customer due diligence (CDD), enhanced due diligence for high-risk customers, transaction monitoring, and suspicious activity reporting. RemitSo's compliance engine covers all requirements.
04
Key Corridors
Top remittance corridors from Netherlands: Morocco, Turkey, Suriname, India, China. RemitSo has pre-integrated payout partners across all major Netherlands corridors — bank transfer, mobile wallets, and cash pickup.
