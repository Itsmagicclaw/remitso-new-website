Legally in 2026

Learn how to send money from China legally in 2026. SAFE rules, $50,000 limits, bank wires, Alipay, WeChat Pay, and compliant transfer methods.

China’s strict foreign exchange regulations make it challenging for individuals and businesses to transfer money internationally. With capital controls in place, sending money abroad requires legal and compliant methods to avoid penalties.

This guide provides a detailed, legally compliant approach to transferring money from China, whether for personal remittances, business transactions, or international payments.

Understanding China’s Foreign Exchange Regulations

China regulates money transfers through the State Administration of Foreign Exchange (SAFE). These controls ensure economic stability but also limit the movement of capital.

Key Regulations for Money Transfers from China:

  • ✓Annual Foreign Exchange Limit: Chinese citizens can exchange up to $50,000 USD per year for overseas transfers.
  • ✓Documented Income Transfers: Transfers above this limit require proof of income, tax documents, and SAFE approval.
  • ✓Restricted Transactions: Unauthorized money transfers via underground banks, unregulated cryptocurrency exchanges, or informal channels are illegal.
  • ✓Business Capital Outflow Restrictions: Companies sending money abroad for investment, trade, or other purposes must obtain regulatory approval.

Understanding these restrictions is crucial to choosing the right legal method for transferring money from China to the UK, USA, or other countries.

Best Legal Methods to Transfer Money from China

Several secure and compliant methods exist to transfer money out of China. Each option has its own requirements, transfer limits, and fees.

1. Bank Wire Transfers:

Wire transfers via Chinese banks are one of the most reliable ways to send large sums abroad. Major banks such as ICBC, Bank of China, and China Construction Bank facilitate international transfers.

Steps to Send Money via Bank Wire Transfer

  • ✓a. Visit Your Bank – Choose a bank that offers international wire transfer services.
  • ✓b. Provide Required Documents – Submit your passport, proof of income, and tax records.
  • ✓c. Exchange RMB to Foreign Currency – The bank will convert the amount, considering exchange rate fluctuations.
  • ✓d. Transfer to International Account – The bank will send the funds to the recipient’s overseas account.

2. Online Payment Platforms (Alipay & WeChat Pay):

Convenient for small transfers with lower fees.

3. International Money Transfer Services:

Services like Western Union, Wise, and PayPal offer secure transfers for personal and business use.

2. Online Payment Platforms (Alipay & WeChat Pay)

For smaller transfers, Alipay and WeChat Pay provide a quick and easy alternative. These platforms allow money transfers to international accounts, but they have strict transfer limits.

Steps to Transfer Money via Alipay & WeChat Pay

  • ✓a. Link Your Bank Account – Connect your Chinese bank account to Alipay or WeChat Pay.
  • ✓b. Initiate the Transfer – Enter the recipient’s details and the amount to send.
  • ✓c. Verify and Approve – Confirm the transaction within the app.

3. International Money Transfer Services (Western Union, Wise, PayPal)

Companies like Western Union, Wise (formerly TransferWise), and PayPal allow individuals to send money internationally. These services are suitable for both personal remittances and small business transactions.

Steps to Transfer Money via International Services

  • ✓a. Create an Account – Sign up with Wise, Western Union, or PayPal.
  • ✓b. Provide Recipient Details – Enter the destination account details.
  • ✓c. Fund the Transfer – Pay via a linked Chinese bank account or digital wallet.
  • ✓d. Confirm and Track Transfer – Receive a tracking number and monitor the payment.

4. Carrying Foreign Currency While Traveling

Another option for small international transfers is exchanging RMB for foreign currency before leaving China.

Steps for Carrying Foreign Currency

  • ✓a. Exchange Money at a Bank – Convert up to $10,000 USD per year at an authorized bank.
  • ✓b. Declare at Customs – If carrying more than $5,000 USD, declare it at customs.
  • ✓c. Deposit in an Overseas Account – Once abroad, deposit the cash into a bank account.
