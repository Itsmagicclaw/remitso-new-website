How to Set Up a Money Transfer
Business in Switzerland

Learn how to start a money transfer business in Switzerland. Get insights on licensing, FINMA compliance, technology setup, and marketing strategies for 2025.

⏱ 12 min read · 🖊 Satish Shrivastava · 📅 2026
⚡ Quick Summary

Learn how to start a money transfer business in Switzerland. Get insights on licensing, FINMA compliance, technology setup, and marketing strategies for 2025.

Switzerland is a global financial hub , playing a key role in international money transfers between Europe, North America, Asia, and the Middle East. With a strong banking system and strict financial regulations, Switzerland is one of the best places to start a money transfer business.

Many people and businesses in Germany, France, the United Kingdom, the United States, India, and the UAE rely on secure international remittance services to send and receive money from Switzerland. With the rise of digital banking and fintech solutions, the demand for fast, affordable, and secure remittance services is higher than ever.

If you're looking to start a remittance business in Switzerland, this guide will walk you through the essential steps—including licensing, compliance, technology setup, and marketing strategies —to help you launch and scale successfully.

1. Why Start a Money Transfer Business in Switzerland?

There are many reasons to start a remittance business in Switzerland , including:

  • ✓Strong demand for money transfers – People and businesses in Switzerland regularly send and receive money internationally.
  • ✓Growing digital payments industry – More people are using money transfer apps and online banking for quick and secure transactions.
  • ✓Favorable business environment – Switzerland offers low corporate tax rates and a stable economy.
  • ✓Trusted financial system – Swiss financial institutions are highly regulated and globally respected.

If you can offer fast, affordable, and secure international money transfer services , your business has a high chance of success.

2. Understanding the Swiss Regulations for Money Transfers

Switzerland has strict financial laws to prevent money laundering and fraud. The Swiss Financial Market Supervisory Authority (FINMA) oversees all financial businesses, including money transfer companies.

a) Do You Need a Money Transfer License?

Yes, if you want to legally send and receive international payments, you must get a Switzerland money transfer license from FINMA.

b) What Are the Licensing Requirements?

To get a FINMA license, you must:

  • ✓Register your company in Switzerland.
  • ✓Meet minimum capital requirements (amount varies based on business size).
  • ✓Have strong security measures to prevent fraud.
  • ✓Follow AML (Anti-Money Laundering) and KYC (Know Your Customer) rules.
  • ✓Report financial transactions regularly to the authorities.

If you don’t follow Swiss financial laws, you could face heavy fines or even business closure.

3. Choosing the Right Business Model

There are different ways to set up a money transfer business in Switzerland. Choose the business model that fits your budget and long-term goals.

a) Bank-Based Money Transfer Service

  • ✓Works through Swiss banks to process money transfers.
  • ✓Pros: Secure and trusted.
  • ✓Cons: Expensive fees, slow processing times.

b) Digital-Only Money Transfer Service

  • ✓Uses a mobile app or website to send money.
  • ✓Pros: Fast, low-cost, scalable.
  • ✓Cons: Requires strong cybersecurity and compliance.

c) Hybrid Model (Online + Cash Pick-Up Locations)

  • ✓Combines digital remittances with physical cash-pickup locations.
  • ✓Pros: Serves both digital users and cash-preferred customers.
  • ✓Cons: More operational costs and logistics.

For Switzerland’s fintech-friendly market, a digital-first money transfer service is the most profitable and scalable option.

4. Setting Up Your Payment Infrastructure

To process secure and efficient international money transfers, you need a strong payment system.

a) Partnering with Banks & Payment Gateways

Work with Swiss banks and international payment providers to:

  • ✓Support multiple currencies (CHF, USD, EUR, GBP, etc.).
  • ✓Offer low transaction fees and competitive exchange rates.
  • ✓Ensure fast and seamless cross-border payments.

b) Building a Secure Money Transfer Platform

A user-friendly money transfer app or website should include:

  • ✓End-to-end encryption for secure payments.
  • ✓Real-time tracking and notifications for customers.
  • ✓Fraud detection systems to stop suspicious transactions.

c) Enabling Cross-Border Transfers

Make sure your platform allows:

  • ✓Money transfers from Switzerland to the EU, USA, India, UAE, and Africa.
  • ✓Multi-currency support for global transactions.
  • ✓Integration with networks like SWIFT & SEPA.

5. Setting Up Competitive Pricing & Transaction Fees

Your pricing strategy should be affordable, transparent, and competitive.

  • ✓Lower transaction fees than traditional banks.
  • ✓Better exchange rates for international transfers.
  • ✓Discounts & promotions for first-time users and loyal customers.

The best way to send money from Switzerland is through low-cost and secure digital remittance services.

6. Marketing Strategies to Scale Your Business

To grow your money transfer business, you need strong marketing strategies.

a) SEO & Content Marketing

  • ✓Optimize for keywords like "best way to send money from Switzerland" & "money transfer business in Switzerland".
  • ✓Write blog content on financial trends and money transfer tips.
  • ✓Use Google Ads & social media marketing to attract users.

b) Partnerships & Affiliate Programs

  • ✓Collaborate with Swiss fintech companies and banks.
  • ✓Offer referral bonuses and cashback deals for new customers.

c) Community-Based Marketing

  • ✓Advertise in expat communities and business networks.
  • ✓Partner with ethnic businesses that frequently send money abroad.

A strong digital presence and strategic partnerships will help you scale your business faster.

7. Ensuring Compliance & Risk Management

Security is critical in the money transfer industry. To prevent fraud:

  • ✓Use AI-driven fraud detection and multi-factor authentication.
  • ✓Monitor transactions in real-time to detect suspicious activity.
  • ✓Regularly update security systems to prevent cyber threats.

Following FINMA regulations ensures long-term success and trust.

Bottom Line

Starting a money transfer business in Switzerland can be highly profitable if you:

  • ✓- Register your company & get a FINMA license.
  • ✓- Build a secure money transfer platform.
  • ✓- Offer competitive pricing & transparent fees.
  • ✓- Implement strong security measures & AML compliance.
  • ✓- Use digital marketing & partnerships to scale your business.
