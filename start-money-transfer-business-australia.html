Regulatory Framework

Understanding AUSTRAC & Australian Regulations

What you need to know before you operate a money transfer business in Australia.

The Regulator
AUSTRAC
Australian Transaction Reports and Analysis Centre. Every remittance business must register with AUSTRAC. Operating without registration is a criminal offence under the AML/CTF Act 2006.
Entity Type
RNP or IRD
You register as a Remittance Network Provider (RNP) if operating a network, or Independent Remittance Dealer (IRD) if operating independently. RemitSo advises which applies to your model.
Key Requirement
AML/CTF Programme
Every registered remittance business must maintain a documented AML/CTF programme — customer due diligence, transaction monitoring, employee training, and record-keeping. RemitSo designs this for you.
Reporting Obligations
SMR, TTR & Annual Report
Suspicious Matter Reports (SMRs), Threshold Transaction Reports (TTRs) for transactions over AUD $10,000, and Annual Compliance Reports to AUSTRAC. RemitSo's platform automates all three.

💳 Australian Payment Gateways — How Senders Fund Transfers
NPP / PayID / Osko
Real-time bank transfer using Australia's New Payments Platform. Instant funding. Familiar to Australian senders. Lowest cost pay-in method available.
Card Processing
Visa and Mastercard debit and credit cards. 3DS2 authentication. Instant funding for urgent transfers. Multiple acquirers for redundancy.
BPAY
Australian bill payment network. Familiar and trusted by Australian consumers for scheduled transfers. Reduces friction for repeat senders.
Direct Debit
Bank account direct debit mandates for recurring and scheduled remittances. Ideal for regular monthly senders — workers sending home on payday.
