What Is a Money Transfer Business in Ghana?

A money transfer business in Ghana is a regulated entity that receives funds from senders and delivers equivalent value to recipients in another country. These businesses — also called Money Service Businesses (MSBs) or Money Transfer Operators (MTOs) — are regulated by BoG and must comply with AML, KYC, and transaction reporting requirements. RemitSo provides the technology and compliance infrastructure so you can run one under your own brand.

Money Transfer Business in Ghana — Licence Requirements

Operating a money transfer business in Ghana without proper authorisation is illegal. BoG oversees the licensing of money transfer operators. Requirements typically include a local entity, minimum capital, AML/CFT policies, a qualified MLRO, and a detailed business plan. RemitSo's compliance advisory service handles every step of this process — or you can bypass the wait entirely using the licensed partner path.
