Regulatory Framework

Germany — What the Law Requires

Understanding your regulatory obligations before you launch protects your business and your customers.

01
Regulator
Bundesanstalt für Finanzdienstleistungsaufsicht (BaFin) is the lead regulatory authority. Oversees authorisation, ongoing supervision, and enforcement for payment institutions operating in Germany.
02
Licensing Framework
ZAG (Zahlungsdiensteaufsichtsgesetz) — Germany's Payment Services Act implementing PSD2. Money transfer businesses need BaFin authorisation as a Payment Institution (PI) or can operate under an EU EMI licence passported from another EEA country.
03
AML Requirements
Full AML/CTF programme required — customer due diligence (CDD), enhanced due diligence for high-risk customers, transaction monitoring, and suspicious activity reporting. RemitSo's compliance engine covers all requirements.
04
Key Corridors
Top remittance corridors from Germany: Turkey, India, Poland, Romania, Syria. RemitSo has pre-integrated payout partners across all major Germany corridors — bank transfer, mobile wallets, and cash pickup.
